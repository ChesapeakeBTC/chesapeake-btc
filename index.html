About The Meetup

We gather monthly to educate, participate, and build. Through open discourse, hands-on learning, and active support for local commerce, we help families, individuals, and small businesses throughout Hampton Roads engage with the free and sovereign technologies shaping our future.

We meet on the 4th Saturday of every month at 2pm in Chesapeake, Virginia. All are welcome, no experience required. Our focus is Bitcoin.

🏘️ Local Pulse

Virginia Enacts HB 798: Unclaimed Crypto Must Be Held In-Kind
Governor Abigail Spanberger signed House Bill 798 into law on Tuesday, rewriting how the Commonwealth handles abandoned digital assets. Starting July 1, cryptocurrency held in customer accounts that show no activity for five years will be presumed abandoned and transferred to state custody — but the state must hold the actual coins, not cash. It also has to wait at least one year before selling. If the rightful owner comes forward inside that window, they get their coins back, not a stale dollar figure. The law is a mixed bag. The state claiming your bitcoin after five years of inactivity is still seizure — the underlying unclaimed property framework is coercive regardless of how the asset is held. The in-kind requirement narrows the damage slightly: Virginia has to hold the actual coins for at least a year before selling, so you won't get handed a stale dollar figure from a forced sale at the bottom. But they can still sell after that window, and the seizure itself is never in question. For anyone in Hampton Roads with a long-dormant exchange account or an inherited wallet they haven't touched, the clock is worth knowing about. The real lesson is the same one it always is: if you hold your own keys, no exchange can report your coins as abandoned to begin with.
Source: Bitcoin Magazine — Virginia Enacts HB 798 · Virginia LIS — HB798

🎓 Bitcoin 101: The Difficulty Adjustment

Fact of the Day: Every 2,016 blocks — roughly two weeks — the Bitcoin network recalibrates how hard it is to mine a new block. This is the difficulty adjustment. The goal is to keep the average time between blocks at ten minutes no matter how much hash power joins or leaves the network. If the last 2,016 blocks came in faster than two weeks, difficulty goes up. Slower, it goes down. No central authority sets this number. Every node computes it independently from the timestamps in the previous retarget window, and they all arrive at the same answer. It's one of the most elegant pieces of Bitcoin's design: a self-balancing clock that no one controls. The next retarget is tomorrow at block 945,504, with an estimated adjustment of −2.76%.

Actionable Tip: Watch the retarget live. Open mempool.757btc.org tomorrow and look at the "Difficulty Adjustment" panel — you'll see the countdown tick to zero and the new difficulty lock in. Seeing the network breathe in real time makes the whole concept stick in a way no article ever will.
Source: Learn Me a Bitcoin — Difficulty · mempool.757btc.org

🌐 The Wider Network

Freedom Tech
OpenSats announced its seventeenth wave of Bitcoin grants this week, adding three first-time recipients and renewing six existing ones. New grantees: JoinMarket NG, a reimplementation of the JoinMarket coinjoin protocol with Nostr-based offer broadcasting and a built-in Neutrino light client; rawBit, a transaction construction tool; and Specter DIY, the open-hardware self-custody project built entirely from off-the-shelf components. Renewals include Payjoin, Bitcoin Safe, Bitshala, and Bitcoin Dev Launchpad. Every dollar in these grants came from voluntary donations to the General Fund — no VCs, no tokens.
Source: OpenSats — Seventeenth Wave of Bitcoin Grants

Product Update
Core Lightning v26.04 release candidate 3 (“Negative Routing Fees”) landed on Sunday, bringing two long-awaited commands to the Lightning Network: splicein and spliceout. Splicing lets a node resize an existing channel — adding or removing funds — without closing and reopening it, saving on-chain fees and preserving uptime. The release also introduces cross-splicing between two channels in a single operation, a payer-note field for xpay, and smaller, faster binaries. If you run a Core Lightning node, the release candidate is available now for testing ahead of the final tag.
