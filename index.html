Who We Are

About The Meetup

We gather monthly to educate, participate, and build. Through open discourse, hands-on learning, and active support for local commerce, we help families, individuals, and small businesses throughout Hampton Roads engage with the free and sovereign technologies shaping our future.

We meet on the 4th Saturday of every month at 2pm in Chesapeake, Virginia. All are welcome, no experience required. Our focus is Bitcoin.

Virginia Strategic Reserve
Virginia's SB 557 would establish the Commonwealth Strategic Cryptocurrency Reserve Fund, authorizing the state treasurer to invest in bitcoin and qualifying digital assets. The bill passed the Senate General Laws and Technology Committee 13–2, joining a growing list of states moving toward sovereign Bitcoin reserves.
Source: Virginia Legislature — SB 557 · Bitcoin Magazine

Bitcoin 101: The Seed Phrase

Fact of the Day: Your Bitcoin wallet doesn't actually "store" bitcoin. It stores a seed phrase — typically 12 or 24 words generated from the BIP-39 standard — that mathematically derives all of your private keys. Whoever holds these words controls the funds. No company, no bank, no government can reset it for you.

Actionable Tip: Write your seed phrase on paper (or stamp it in metal) and store it somewhere safe and offline. Never type it into a website. Never photograph it. Never store it in cloud notes. Test your backup by restoring it on a separate device before loading significant funds.
Source: bitcoin.org — Securing Your Wallet · BIP-39 Specification

The Wider Network

Freedom Tech
The 256 Foundation funds open-source hardware and software projects aimed at breaking Bitcoin mining free from closed-source control. Current grants include Mujina, a next-gen open-source mining firmware, the Libre Board, an open ASIC control board, the Ember One hashboard, and Hydra Pool, a self-hosted mining pool. Together they form a fully open Bitcoin mining stack.
Source: 256 Foundation — Grants
