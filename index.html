Who We Are

About The Meetup

We gather monthly to educate, participate, and build. Through open discourse, hands-on learning, and active support for local commerce, we help families, individuals, and small businesses throughout Hampton Roads engage with the free and sovereign technologies shaping our future.

We meet on the 4th Saturday of every month at 2pm in Chesapeake, Virginia. All are welcome, no experience required. Our focus is Bitcoin.

🏘️ Local Pulse

Virginia Beach Farmers Market Turns 50 Tomorrow
The Virginia Beach Farmers Market on Dam Neck Road is throwing a 50th Birthday Bash this Saturday, April 18, from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Expect vendors, food trucks, live music, children's activities, and free birthday cake while it lasts. Fifty years of connecting local growers directly to the people who eat their food is worth celebrating. The market runs year-round with an assortment of agriculture shops and small businesses, and the birthday event is open to everyone. If you're in the 757, it's a good excuse to buy something from the person who made it, skip the supply chain, and put money straight into a neighbor's pocket. That's the circular economy in miniature — and it doesn't require anyone's permission.
Source: City of Virginia Beach — Farmers Market

🎓 Bitcoin 101: Running a Full Node

Fact of the Day: A full node is a computer running Bitcoin software that independently validates every transaction and every block against the consensus rules. It doesn't trust anyone else's copy of the ledger. It downloads the entire blockchain, checks every signature, verifies every output hasn't been spent twice, and rejects anything that breaks the rules — no matter who mined it. When you run a node, you're not just watching the network. You're enforcing the rules yourself. That's what "don't trust, verify" actually means in practice. There are roughly 20,000 reachable nodes on the network right now, and each one arrived at the same chain tip independently. No one told them to agree. They just ran the same math.

Action Item: You can run a full node on a Raspberry Pi, an old laptop, or any machine with about 1 TB of storage and a decent internet connection. Bitcoin.org's full node guide walks you through setting up Bitcoin Core step by step. Once it syncs, you'll have your own verified copy of every transaction that's ever happened on the network. Point your wallet at it and you stop relying on anyone else's server for your balance or broadcast.
Source: Bitcoin.org — Running a Full Node · Learn Me a Bitcoin — Nodes

🌐 The Wider Network

Freedom Tech
Stacked, a four-person Bitcoin company in New Zealand formerly known as Lightning Pay, just launched a self-custodial Lightning wallet. That matters because every other major exchange in the country has gone the opposite direction. EasyCrypto got bought out by SwyFTX and shut down, funneling users into a custodial platform. Sharesies doesn't even let you withdraw crypto. Stacked's wallet uses Breez and Spark SDKs under the hood, lets users buy bitcoin on a schedule with Autostack (a DCA feature), and pay bills or rent in bitcoin with fiat settlement on the other end. They're also building the "Bitcoin Basin" circular economy in Queenstown, with a growing number of bitcoin-accepting merchants. It's the kind of project that makes "be your own bank" less of a slogan and more of a lifestyle.
Source: Bitcoin Magazine — Stacked Launches Self-Custodial Lightning Wallet

Product Update
Bitcoin Core v31.0 release candidate 4 dropped last week and is now available for testing. This is the fourth RC for the next major release, which follows v30.2 and brings cluster mempool improvements among other changes. The Review Club hosted a dedicated testing session on April 8 to walk contributors through the RC Testing Guide. If you run a node, testing release candidates before the final tag is one of the most direct ways to contribute to Bitcoin's development without writing a single line of code. Binaries are available on bitcoincore.org.
Source: GitHub — Bitcoin Core v31.0rc4 · Bitcoin Core Review Club — v31 RC Testing
