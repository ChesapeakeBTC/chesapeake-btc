About The Meetup

We gather monthly to educate, participate, and build. Through open discourse, hands-on learning, and active support for local commerce, we help families, individuals, and small businesses throughout Hampton Roads engage with the free and sovereign technologies shaping our future.

We meet on the 4th Saturday of every month at 2pm in Chesapeake, Virginia. All are welcome, no experience required. Our focus is Bitcoin.

🏘️ Local Pulse

Community Corner
Sunday morning in the 757. A good day to think about where your money goes. Every dollar spent at a chain store leaves the region. Every dollar spent at a local shop, a local farm stand, a neighbor's side business stays here, circulating through the community and strengthening the people around you. That's the circular economy in its simplest form, and it works whether you're paying in dollars or sats. Bitcoin adds something powerful to this idea: the ability to transact directly, peer to peer, without a processor skimming three percent off the top. Imagine a farmers market where every vendor accepts Lightning payments. No chargebacks, no monthly fees, no corporate middleman. That future isn't science fiction. It's being built right now in communities like ours. The Causeway meets next Saturday, April 25, at 2 p.m. at Accordo Chiropractic in Chesapeake. If you've been thinking about what sovereignty looks like in practice, this is a good place to start the conversation.

🎓 Bitcoin 101: The Lightning Network

Fact of the Day: Bitcoin's base layer processes roughly seven transactions per second. That's by design — security and decentralization come first. But buying a coffee shouldn't require the same settlement guarantees as buying a house. That's where the Lightning Network comes in. Lightning is a second-layer protocol built on top of Bitcoin. Two parties open a payment channel by locking bitcoin into a shared multisig transaction on the base chain. Once the channel is open, they can send payments back and forth instantly by updating the balance between them, without broadcasting anything to the blockchain. When they're done, they close the channel and the final balances settle on-chain. The real power is routing. You don't need a direct channel with every person you want to pay. If Alice has a channel with Bob, and Bob has a channel with Carol, Alice can pay Carol through Bob. The network finds a path automatically. The result is near-instant payments with fees measured in fractions of a penny, all secured by Bitcoin's base layer.

Action Item: Download Phoenix Wallet (available on Android and iOS from phoenix.acinq.co). It's self-custodial, meaning you hold your own keys, and it handles Lightning channel management automatically. Once installed, back up your seed phrase, then try receiving a small payment. You'll see how fast Lightning settles compared to waiting for on-chain confirmations. This is the payment layer that makes Bitcoin work for everyday transactions.
Source: Lightning Network · Bitcoin.org — Innovation

🌐 The Wider Network

Freedom Tech
The MIT Bitcoin Expo wrapped up its 2026 edition last week with the theme "Freedom for All." Held April 11–12 on the MIT campus in Cambridge, the event featured talks from Bitcoin Core maintainer Ava Chow, Lightning Network developer Tadge Dryja, Cashu creator Calle, and HRF-backed digital rights advocate Roya Mahboob, whose keynote addressed "Bitcoin and the Right to Financial Existence." Technical sessions covered SwiftSync (a method for accelerating initial block download), quantum computing defenses for Bitcoin, and zero-knowledge proof applications. A 36-hour hackathon ran alongside the talks, with 70% of the prize pool distributed to community-selected projects. The Expo has been running since 2014, making it the longest-running university-hosted Bitcoin event in the world.
Source: MIT Bitcoin Expo 2026

Product Update
Sparrow Wallet released v2.2.0, bringing several features that matter for self-custody. The Send tab now includes a Recent Blocks view so you can see current fee conditions before broadcasting. Sparrow added support for pay-to-anchor (P2A) outputs, which are part of Bitcoin Core's evolving transaction relay improvements. BIP-322 message signing now works via QR code and file, making it easier to prove ownership of an address when using a hardware wallet. The update also implements TOFU (trust on first use) certificate pinning for TLS connections to your own Bitcoin Core node, adding a layer of security against man-in-the-middle attacks. If you run Sparrow on Linux, note that the package has been renamed from "sparrow" to "sparrowwallet" — you may need to manually uninstall the old package.
Source: GitHub — Sparrow Wallet v2.2.0
