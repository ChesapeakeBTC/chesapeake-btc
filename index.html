Who We Are

About The Meetup

We gather monthly to educate, participate, and build. Through open discourse, hands-on learning, and active support for local commerce, we help families, individuals, and small businesses throughout Hampton Roads engage with the free and sovereign technologies shaping our future.

We meet on the 4th Saturday of every month at 2pm in Chesapeake, Virginia. All are welcome, no experience required. Our focus is Bitcoin.

🏘️ Local Pulse

Community Corner: The Case for Buying Local
Hampton Roads has no shortage of small, independent businesses — the butcher shop, the mechanic, the local roaster, the neighborhood hardware store. These aren't just conveniences. They're the circulatory system of a local economy. When you spend money there, it stays close: rent to a local landlord, wages to a neighbor, stock to a regional supplier. Now imagine that same loop running on bitcoin — final transactions, minimal fees, no payment processor cut. That's available today. If you run a business in Hampton Roads or know one worth spotlighting, we want to hear about it. And if you're a customer, ask: "Do you take bitcoin?" One conversation at a time.

🎓 Bitcoin 101: The Mempool

Fact of the Day: When you send a bitcoin transaction, it doesn't land on the blockchain right away. It enters the mempool — short for "memory pool" — a holding area maintained independently by every Bitcoin node on the network. Miners pull transactions from their mempool to fill the next block, generally selecting those with the highest fee per byte of data. Because every node runs its own mempool, there's no single global queue — just thousands of overlapping lists that converge through consensus. Right now the network is light: fees are sitting at 1–3 sats per virtual byte.

Actionable Tip: Bookmark mempool.757btc.org — our community's own mempool explorer, run by 757btc. Before you send any transaction, check the current fee environment. A light mempool like today's is the best time to consolidate UTXOs, open Lightning channels, or move funds you've been putting off. Don't pay priority fees when the highway is empty.
Source: Learn Me a Bitcoin — Memory Pool · mempool.757btc.org

🌐 The Wider Network

Freedom Tech
The Human Rights Foundation announced 1.5 billion satoshis in new grants through its Bitcoin Development Fund, supporting 26 projects in Q1 2026. Recipients include work on Bitcoin Core P2P privacy improvements, JoinMarket-NG (a new CoinJoin implementation for financial privacy), and Banxaas — a peer-to-peer bitcoin exchange for West Africa that operates without accounts or custodians. These grants target the tools that let dissidents, journalists, and activists move money without asking permission.
Source: Bitcoin Magazine — HRF's Bitcoin Development Fund Supports 26 Projects

Product Update
Bitcoin Core 30.2 is the current stable release. It's a maintenance update in the v30 series with reliability and stability improvements. If you're running a full node and haven't updated, now's a good time. The v31.0 release candidate (rc4) is in testing — final release expected in the coming weeks.
Source: Bitcoin Core — v30.2 Release Notes
